Who We Are

 

Judith W. Andrucki

A specialist in family law, Judith W. Andrucki received her J.D. from the University of Maine School of Law in 1979 and practiced with the firm of Marshall, Raymond and Beliveau, as well as its’ successor firm, Isaacson & Raymond, until she formed her own firm of Hark Andrucki. In 1999 Hark Andrucki merged with Troubh Heisler where she practiced until July 2007 when she opened The Law Offices of Judith W. Andrucki. From 2008- 2013 she was a partner in the firm of Andrucki & Mitchell. She is now the managing partner of Andrucki & Associates, Attorneys at Law.

In 1991, in addition to practicing law, Judith supervised the Family Law Clinic and taught Family Law and Evidence at the University of Maine School of Law. She is a member of the Family Law Section of the Maine Bar Association and has presented numerous Continuing Legal Education programs on issues concerning the litigation of custody disputes, alternate dispute resolution and mediation, the role of the Guardian Ad Litem, and the determination of employability and income capacity. In 1997, Governor Angus King appointed Judith to serve on the University of Maine System Board of Trustees where she served two full terms, concluding in 2007. Judith also serves as a mediator and referee with respect to family law disputes. Ms. Andrucki currently serves on the Board of Directors of St. Mary’s Health System and the Maine Music Society.

You may contact Attorney Andrucki by calling our office at (207) 777-4600, and speaking with her legal assistant, Michele.

Mara R. King

Mara HunterMara R. King, an associate at Andrucki & Associates, holds a B.A. degree from Wheaton College in Massachusetts and received her J.D. from the University Of Maine School Of Law.

Prior to working in the legal field, Mara worked for the New York State Office of Federal Affairs in Washington, DC; the Robert F. Kennedy Memorial as Deputy to the Executive Director in their Washington DC office and Director of the San Francisco Robert F. Kennedy Fellows AmeriCorps program. In 1999, she returned to Maine where she worked in Lewiston for New Beginnings Transitional Living Program and the State of Maine’s Department of Health and Human Services as a children services caseworker.

Mara is a founding Board Member of the Maine Alliance for Drug Treatment Courts, a local non-profit organization created to support and enhance services to Maine Drug Treatment Court clients and to promote community understanding of the benefits of these collaborative courts. Mara is a volunteer for the Volunteer Lawyers Project providing free legal services for the Court House Assistance Project (CHAP) at the Lewiston District Court. She has been an active volunteer for CHAP since the program began in Lewiston in 2009. Mara was a member of the Justice for Children Task force from 2007-2010.

Mara is admitted to practice in the State of Maine. She is a member the Androscoggin County Bar Association and the Maine State Bar Association. Mara practices in Southern and Central Maine in the areas of Parental Rights and Responsibilities, Divorce, Protection from Abuse, Guardianship of Minors, Adoption, and Qualified Domestic Relations Orders. She is also a rostered Guardian ad Litem in the State of Maine.

Raised in Bangor, Mara now lives with her husband and family in Androscoggin County.

You may contact Attorney King by calling our office at (207) 777-4600, and speaking with her legal assistant, Michele.

Elsye B. Segovias

Elyse SegoviasElyse joined Andrucki & Associates in 2011. Prior to working at Andrucki & Associates, she practiced on her own in York and Cumberland Counties in the areas of Family Law, Juvenile Defense and Child Protection. Elyse now focuses on divorce, parental rights & responsibilities, adoption, and issues facings same-sex couples, including divorce. She practices in Cumberland, Androscoggin, Kennebec, York and Oxford counties. She is admitted to the Maine Bar, and is a member of the Androscoggin County Bar Association, and the New Lawyers’, Women’s Law, and Family Law sections of the Maine Bar Association. She is a rostered Guardian ad Litem. Elyse is also a volunteer lawyer at the Volunteer Lawyers’ Project Court House Assistance Program at the Lewiston District Court.

Raised in Saco, Maine, Elyse now lives with her wife, daughter, and dog in Cumberland County.

You may contact Attorney Segovias by calling our office at (207) 777-4600, and speaking with her legal assistant, Michele.
